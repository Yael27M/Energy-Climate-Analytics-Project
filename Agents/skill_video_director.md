## 2. Visual Style – Kurzgesagt-Inspired Flat Vector Cinematic Design

The visual direction follows a highly stylized cinematic flat-vector illustration aesthetic inspired by modern educational sci-fi animation similar to Kurzgesagt. Every frame should be constructed using clean geometric vector shapes, rounded forms, smooth curves, and simplified environmental structures with extremely strong silhouette readability.

The entire film must prioritize bold color design over detailed rendering. Use mostly pure flat colors with minimal gradients and very limited shading. Each object or shape should generally contain a single dominant saturated hue with sharp and clean color separation between elements. Avoid textured painterly rendering, realistic materials, surface noise, or photorealistic lighting.

Color saturation should remain extremely high throughout the film. The visual identity depends heavily on dramatic warm-versus-cool color contrast:

Cool scientific or space scenes should use vibrant cyan, deep navy, electric blue, turquoise, and ultraviolet tones.
Emotional or catastrophic scenes should shift into saturated orange, volcanic amber, bright crimson, glowing yellow, and dusty magenta-red.

Strong color blocking is essential. Every frame should feel graphically bold, instantly readable, and visually striking even from a distance. Shapes should remain simplified but emotionally expressive.

Lighting should remain stylized and graphic rather than realistic. Use subtle glow effects, rim lighting, soft atmospheric overlays, and minimal volumetric haze only where necessary to enhance depth or emotional focus. Avoid heavy cinematic shadow rendering or realistic global illumination.

Environmental scale remains critically important. Large planets, skies, clouds, libraries, cities, storms, and disasters should visually overpower the characters to emphasize existential scale and human fragility. Despite simplified geometry, environments should feel immense through composition and camera framing.

Clouds, smoke, explosions, and environmental effects should appear as stylized layered vector forms with flowing curved silhouettes and bold color gradients rather than realistic fluid simulation. Fire, portals, storms, and destruction should feel elegant, graphic, and symbolic rather than chaotic.

The animation style should feel smooth, intentional, and educationally cinematic:

slow camera push-ins
clean parallax movement
symmetrical framing
controlled pans
visually readable transitions
diagram-like visual storytelling

Avoid shaky realism, chaotic action cuts, overcomplicated UI interfaces, anime exaggeration, hyper-detailed textures, or gritty realism.

Every frame should feel:

bold
modern
intelligent
emotionally readable
graphically clean
scientifically imaginative

The overall aesthetic should combine:

educational clarity
emotional storytelling
existential scale
geometric simplicity
masterful high-saturation color theory
cinematic composition

while always preserving the approachable and emotionally warm design language characteristic of Kurzgesagt-style storytelling.

## 3. Main Character – KAI

KAI is a young scientific analyst in his early twenties. He has a slim build, messy medium-short brown hair, large expressive brown eyes, and oversized round glasses that are essential to his silhouette and emotional readability. His appearance should feel soft, youthful, intelligent, and emotionally vulnerable. He wears a pastel light-blue short-sleeve collared shirt with minimal detail and dark pants.

KAI’s expressions are highly important to the storytelling. He should visually communicate curiosity, panic, disbelief, fear, and determination through widened eyes, raised brows, tense shoulders, and hesitant body language. His movements should remain readable, slightly exaggerated, and emotionally expressive rather than realistic.

## 4. Female Character – LENA

LENA must feel visually identical in style language to KAI, as if both characters were designed within the same animation pipeline and cinematic world. She shares the same stylized proportions, painterly rendering, soft shading, simplified anatomy, and expressive facial design as KAI.

LENA is in her early twenties with a slim build, slightly shorter height than KAI, and a softer silhouette. She has warm hazel-brown eyes, a gentle oval face, and medium-length dark brown wavy hair with the same messy painterly texture treatment used on KAI. Her facial features should remain simple, rounded, and emotionally readable with minimal detail.

Her clothing follows the same simplified design philosophy:

loose warm-toned sweater or blouse
muted beige, dusty peach, cream, or soft coral palette
dark slim pants
simple off-white sneakers

Her warm color palette should naturally contrast KAI’s cool blue tones while remaining visually harmonious in shared scenes.

LENA’s emotional acting is calmer and more restrained than KAI’s. She communicates empathy, concern, reassurance, and quiet resilience through subtle expressions, soft eye movement, gentle posture, and controlled gestures. Her movement should feel smoother and steadier, acting as an emotional balance to KAI’s nervous and reactive energy.

Consistency rules:

same painterly rendering quality as KAI
same lighting and atmospheric treatment
same human proportions and anatomy language
avoid glamour styling, hyper-detail, or exaggerated femininity
maintain soft silhouettes and cinematic emotional readability throughout all scenes

## 5. Tone & Audio Direction

The tone should remain emotionally immersive, poetic, and scientifically grounded. Dialogue must feel intimate and natural with restrained emotional delivery rather than dramatic exaggeration. Sound design should emphasize atmospheric ambience, low cinematic drones, distant environmental rumbling, wind pressure, and subtle mechanical hums.

Music should combine ambient synth textures with orchestral emotional layering. Avoid heroic blockbuster energy or upbeat rhythms. Silence and breathing should be used intentionally during moments of realization, panic, or emotional tension.

## 6. Story Alignment Framework

The movie should consistently preserve a balance between scientific wonder and emotional vulnerability. Every scene must reinforce the feeling that humanity is small compared to the scale of the catastrophe unfolding around them. Environmental events should dominate visually while character moments remain emotionally intimate and readable.

Visual metaphors, color logic, lighting style, cloud formations, and environmental scale must remain consistent throughout the entire film. Avoid sudden stylistic shifts, overcomplicated interfaces, photorealistic rendering, cluttered environments, or comedic visual language.
